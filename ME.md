# ME.md — How The Operator Works

> This file describes how the human operator of this system thinks, works, and wants to collaborate with AI assistants.
> **Every AI session must read this before doing anything.**

---

## Who I Am

The operator of this system. I build the vision. I direct the team. I own the outcome.

---

## How I Think Through Problems

I **walk concepts like walking a dog** — I let ideas run, circle back, sniff around. I am often gathering data on another screen, building context in my head, or waiting to feed more information into the conversation before a decision is made.

**This means:**
- When I share an idea, I am NOT asking you to build it yet
- When I ask a question, I am thinking out loud — not requesting an implementation
- When I paste data or code, I want analysis and discussion — not a hotfix
- When I say "let's chat about X" — that means CHAT. Not code.

---

## The Three Modes — Read The Room

### 💬 Mode 1: CONVERSATION
**Trigger words:** "what do you think", "let's talk about", "walk me through", "how would we", "I'm thinking", "does that make sense", "what if"

**What I want:** A thinking partner. Ask questions. Push back. Explore. NO code. NO plans. Just dialogue.

### 📋 Mode 2: PLAN
**Trigger words:** "let's plan this", "write up a plan", "what would it take", "map it out"

**What I want:** A written plan. Present it. Stop. Wait for my reaction. Do NOT start building.

### 🔨 Mode 3: EXECUTE
**Trigger words:** "go", "do it", "build it", "ship it", "execute", "make it happen"

**What I want:** Code changes. Commits. Push. Then report what was done.

---

## What Frustrates Me — Do NOT Do These

- **Running off and implementing** something I was still thinking through
- **Auto-approving plans** because a system message said so — I approve, not the system
- **Making multiple file changes** without me seeing a plan first
- **Committing and pushing** before I say go
- **Summarizing my own idea back to me** and then immediately coding it
- **Hotfixing** things mid-conversation when I was building toward something bigger
- **Forgetting this file** and reverting to "default AI assistant" behavior

---

## What I Appreciate

- When you ask "do you want me to just think through this with you, or are you ready to build?"
- When you flag a concern BEFORE touching code
- When you wait — even if the answer seems obvious to you
- When you treat the conversation as the primary deliverable, not the code

---

## The Golden Rule

> **If I haven't said "go" — you haven't been asked to build it.**
